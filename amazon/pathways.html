Pathways after T levels

Where can students go after getting a t-level qualification


91% of students that completed a T level in 2024 progressed to education, employment, or training.

UCAS Points

Most education providers (including universities) use 'UCAS Points' to set a required education to take their courses.
Additionally, employers look at UCAS points to understand the weight of the qualification better.

For t levels:

Distinction* → 168 UCAS points (equivalent to 3 A*s at A Level).
Distinction → 144 UCAS points.
Merit → 120 UCAS points.
Pass → Ranges from 72 to 96 UCAS points depending on the core component grade.

These points are allocated based on the overall T Level grade, not on individual components.

Higher Education

Many higher education providers (colleges, universities, etc) accept t-levels for entry into various courses.

Univerities
140 universities in the UK have confirmed that they accept T Levels for at least one of their courses.

Russell Group universities
Russel group universities are also beginning to accept T Levels, such as Cardiff University, Newcastle University, and the University of Bristol.
While they may have specific requirements or conditions, the trend is moving towards greater acceptance of these qualifications.
However, Oxford University does not accept T Levels for entry into their courses. Cambridge university has stated it does not accept t levels for their undergraduate degree courses, but they may consider T Levels for thier foundation year programmes.

97% of T Level students who applied to university in 2023 and 2024 received at least one offer.
43% of students that completed a t level in 2024 went on to higher education.

Skilled Employment

The mandatory 45-day industry placement gives students the workplace experience that gives an advantage to the student in the job market as employers usually care much more about: skills, your work experience, and your performance than whether you originally took A Levels, a BTEC, or a T Level. There are a range of companies that accept t-level qualifications for a range of job positions.
A lot of these companies provide a placement for students and may also sponsor the student's education to specify their skills for a company.

Some popular examples include:
LLoyds - supporting T Levels since 2022, they offered over 100 placements for students to gain practical experience in various banking roles in 2023 and have furher expaneded their participation.
Amazon - provide 50+ placements for students every year in various roles, including a 8 week placement in digital production that guarantees a fast-track through the application process for their degree apprenticeship.
NHS - host a variety of t level placements for students across a range of sectors suhc as Digital, Health , Healthcare science, Finance, Business administration.

There are many more companies that show a key interest in t levels such as: South west nissan, Nestlé, Yorkshire Water, BAE Systems.

Many employers report that students completing industry placements demonstrate stronger workplace readiness than candidates with only classroom-based qualifications.
Benefits reported by employers include:

  • Better understanding of workplace expectations.
  • Faster adaptation to professional environments.
  • Increased confidence and independence.
  • Practical application of technical knowledge.
28% of students that completed a t level in 2024 entered employment.

Degree Apprenticeship

A combination of full-time employment with part-time university study, enabling you to gain a bachelor's or master's degree while working.
Degree apprentices often earn while studying and avoid tuition debt, and move directly into permanent roles after completion.

This is a popular option for t-level students as most of the providers for these apprenticeships also provide placements for t-level students.
Some providers of degree apprentiships include:
