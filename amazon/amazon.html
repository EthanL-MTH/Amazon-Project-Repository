T Levels With Amazon

Why teaming up with Amazon for your education is the way to go!

Amazon is one of the UK's leading employers supporting T-Level industry placements.
The programme was designed to help students make a smooth transition from education into employment by providing real workplace experience, and requries no previous experience from students.
Initially focused on Digital T-Levels, the programme has expanded to Business, Engineering and Creative pathways, and has increased from 6 placements to 100 in just 3 years!

💼What makes a placement at Amazon special?

1.Students are supported throughout their placement by dedicated mentors, buddies and placement managers to help students build confidence.
2.Students take part in team projects, individual assignments, charitable initiatives and business
challenges that allow them to apply the knowledge gained during their T-Level studies to real business situations.
3.Amazon has created progression routes from T-Level placements directly into apprenticeship opportunities.
4.Provides students with an opportunity to work within a huge global company (310 million+ active users wordwide), and learn skills they can take all across the world.

📚Recruiment Process + More infomation

Amazon placements can run from 3 weeks to 9 weeks (45 working days) depending on the placement you are doing.
Amazon uses a recruitment process similar to its apprenticeship programme which includes:

  • Application form
  • CV screening
  • Motivational questions
  • Assessment Centre

This means students gain experience of a professional recruitment process before entering full-time employment.
However, some placements can be applied for through your educational provider such as college.
In this scenario, the college will maintain communication with Amazon and send them your cv.
Further steps will be taken from there.

🌍Youth Support

Amazon has supported education and skills development for youths to help them build employability skills such as teamwork, collaboration, and problem solving.
Some examples include:

  • Offering free coding courses, virtual school trips, career talks, and AI/cloud computing resources for schools across the UK.
  • Step Up Work Experience Programme, which gives young people aged 14–25 hands-on workplace experience.
  • Supported Internships for young people with Special Educational Needs and Disabilities (SEND), helping participants transition into employment.
  • Career Choice programme, in which Amazon funds employees' education and training in areas such as software development, cybersecurity, engineering, healthcare, and HGV driving.
    In the UK, eligible employees can receive up to £8,000 in tuition and course fees over four years.
  • Amazon University Talent, graduate schemes helping recent graduates begin careers in technology, business, and operations.


    • The company's wider youth programmes show strong outcomes, with over 80% of supported interns progressing into employment and 74% securing jobs directly with Amazon.
    These statistics demonstrate that Amazon is not only investing in education but also creating measurable career opportunities for young people.

🤝Amazon supported internships

work-based study programmes for young people with Special Education Needs and Disabilities.

Launched in 2021, Amazon’s programme has grown to support almost 300 young people in partnership with national charity DFN Project SEARCH.
It supports young people aged 18 to 24 with SEND who have an Education, Health, and Care Plan (EHCP) or equivalent.

This programme combine classroom education and practical work experience in various roles all within an Amazon fulfilment centre to help develop student's English and maths proficiencies, as well as CV writing and other workplace skills.

Over 80% of participants move into permanent employment.
